**Author:** Product/Strategy
**Status:** Draft

## 1. Overview

This document formally defines the user personas interacting with the Custom Costing Tool v1, outlining their profiles, usage contexts, constraints, and primary user journeys. It builds upon the PRD and target UX goals, providing a human-centric view of how the system is used in practice.

---

## 2. Persona 1: The Designer (Primary User)

### 2.1 Profile & Context
- **Role:** HomeLane or DesignCafe Designer.
- **Context:** Operates "at the table" inside a DesignCafe or HomeLane showroom/experience center. The customer may be physically present sitting right next to them, or on a video/audio call. The designer accesses the custom costing tool on their laptop via a web browser. They are focused on selling the design and closing the deal, not on the underlying math of manufacturing.
- **Goal:** To generate a trustworthy, customer-ready quote for a custom furniture unit in minutes without breaking the flow of the sales conversation.

### 2.2 Constraints
- **Time Pressure:** Cannot afford a 3-4 day turnaround for quotes; needs an answer in < 5 minutes.
- **Customer Visibility:** Since they are with the customer, the screen is often visible. They cannot expose internal margins, markup calculations, or raw cost structures.
- **Tool Fatigue:** They use SketchUp, Foyr, and CRM tools daily. They will not adopt a tool that requires them to re-draw or learn complex new CAD software. The tool must accept their existing outputs (PDFs, raw photos of sketches).

### 2.3 User Journey: Creating & Presenting a Quote (Happy Path)
1. **Trigger:** The designer finalizes a custom design with the customer (e.g., via SketchUp). The customer shares the design PDF or a raw photo image of a sketch via WhatsApp with the designer.
2. **Upload & Extract:** The designer uploads the document to the Custom Costing Tool. They watch the progress bar as the AI extracts dimensions, finishes, and premium features, and classifies the document type.
3. **Review & Confirm:** The designer reads the **Human-Readable Summary** generated by the AI to understand its assumptions. They review the extracted fields on the right pane against the document preview on the left. They acknowledge any "low confidence" warnings or "multi-unit" banners, and make manual corrections if the AI missed a detail. (If they uploaded a Pinterest inspiration photo, the system blocks the quote and asks for a proper drawing).
4. **Presentation Mode (Enabled by Default):** Because the workflow is run entirely in front of the customer, **Presentation Mode is enabled by default at all times**. This ensures internal margin percentages and raw cost data are hidden, leaving only the branded, customer-facing price and feature list visible. The designer can choose to disable it manually to view the internal costing if they wish to do so.
5. **Upsell Conversation:** The designer dynamically toggles premium features (e.g., "add fluted glass") in the UI. The customer immediately sees the incremental price impact.
6. **Finalize & Export:** Once the customer agrees to the price, the designer clicks "Generate Quote PDF". They download the clean, customer-facing PDF and hand it to the customer.

## 3. Persona 2: Senior Designer / Regional Manager (Approver)

### 3.1 Profile & Context
- **Role:** Senior Design Lead or Regional Sales Manager.
- **Context:** Operates from a desk, managing multiple designers across a region. They review deals to ensure margins are protected and discounts are not excessive.
- **Goal:** To quickly review and approve high-value or high-variance custom quotes without becoming a bottleneck.

### 3.2 Constraints
- **Volume:** They cannot spend 30 minutes manually recalculating a custom quote to verify its accuracy. They need immediate visibility into the underlying BOM and margin structure.

### 3.3 User Journey: Quote Approval
1. **Trigger:** A designer generates a quote that exceeds a predefined value or discount threshold. The tool automatically flags this quote as "Pending Approval".
2. **Dashboard Review:** The Senior Designer logs into the tool's dashboard and opens the pending quote.
3. **Audit:** They review the internal cost sheet, examining the BOM, applied margins, confidence buffers, and the AI's source extraction to ensure the quote is structurally sound.
4. **Decision:** They approve the quote directly within the application, changing its status to "Approved," which automatically notifies the original designer that the quote is ready for the customer.

## 4. Persona 3: D2M Analyst & Procurement Analyst (Data Owners)

### 4.1 Profile & Context
- **Role:** 
  - **D2M Analyst:** Owns the Feature Library (manufacturing rules, wastage %, labour deltas).
  - **Procurement Analyst:** Owns the Master Rate DB (material, hardware, and overhead rates).
- **Context:** Back-office administrative users. They act as the source of truth for the deterministic costing engine.
- **Goal:** To ensure the tool's underlying math reflects current market reality and manufacturing capabilities.

### 4.2 Constraints
- **Ad-Hoc Workflow:** Updates are not predictable. They do not operate on a fixed software release schedule; they update data ad-hoc based on offline communications from senior management or vendor pricing changes.
- **No Engineering Dependency:** They must be able to update rates and feature models via an admin interface without requiring an engineer to deploy code.

### 4.3 User Journey: Ad-Hoc Master Data Updates
1. **Trigger:** Senior management informs the analyst offline about a strategic change (e.g., a new vendor agreement changes the rate for BWP ply, or a new premium feature like "Arch Cutouts" is approved for standard quoting).
2. **Login & Locate:** The analyst logs into the tool's Admin interface.
3. **Update Record:** 
   - *Procurement:* Creates a new effective-dated row for the material rate in the Master Rate DB, deprecating the old rate.
   - *D2M:* Adjusts the wastage percentage or labour multiplier for a specific premium feature in the Feature Library interface.
4. **Publish:** They save the changes. The system immediately uses the new parameters for all subsequent quotes, while preserving the historical rate sets for past quotes to ensure auditability.
